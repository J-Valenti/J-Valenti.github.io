CREAMY BUTTER CHICKEN - WITH A FIBER TWIST

Rich, creamy, and full of warm spices, this butter chicken has all the comfort of the classic dish with a secret fiber boost. Puréed cannellini beans melt seamlessly into the sauce, making it extra velvety without changing the flavor. Perfect for scooping up with rice or naan, it’s a cozy weeknight dinner that’s both nourishing and indulgent.

PREP TIME: 15 MINUTES     COOK TIME: 25 MINUTES

TOTAL TIME: 40 MINUTES     YEILDS: 5

INGREDIENTS

CHICKEN MARINADE

  • 1.5 pounds chicken breast (about 2 large breasts), cubed
  • 1/4 cup plain whole yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons garam masala
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons cumin
  • 1 tablespoon coriander
  • 1/2 tablespoon turmeric
  • 2 teaspoons fresh ginger, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne powder (add more for spiciness)
  • 2 tablespoons neutral oil or ghee

THE SAUCE

  • 1 tablespoon neutral oil or ghee
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 (15 oz) can cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup milk (plus an extra splash if needed)
  • 2/3 cup heavy cream (or half and half, whole milk)
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (or to taste)

FOR GARNISH

  • chopped cilantro
  • yogurt

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, mix together the chicken, yogurt, garam masala, salt, cumin, coriander, turmeric, ginger, garlic, paprika, cayenne, and oil or ghee. Toss until evenly coated. Optional: Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight.
  2. Cook the Chicken: Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken in a single layer and cook for 7-8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until fully cooked through and golden brown on the outside. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  3. Start the Sauce: In the same skillet over medium heat, add 1 tablespoon oil or ghee. Add the tomato paste and cook for 2–3 minutes, stirring often, until it deepens in color and becomes rich and fragrant.
  4. Blend Beans & Milk: In a blender, combine the drained cannellini beans with milk and purée until completely smooth and creamy. Add an extra splash of milk if needed to loosen the texture.
  5. Build the Sauce: Stir the tomato sauce into the skillet with the cooked-down tomato paste. Add the bean purée, then pour in the heavy cream. Stir well, then finish with the butter, letting it melt into the sauce. Add sugar and stir together.
  6. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet, tossing to coat it in the sauce. Simmer for 2–3 minutes to bring everything together. Taste and adjust salt, spice level, and sugar to your liking.
  7. Garnish with chopped cilantro and a dollop of yogurt. Serve warm with basmati rice or naan.
